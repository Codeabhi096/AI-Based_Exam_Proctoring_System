Compliance and Privacy FAQs
What are some of the key focus areas in compliance?

Privacy and data security are always top of mind for MyProctor. The laws are changing quickly in this area all across the globe, and we're constantly enhancing our policies and practices to ensure we are protecting our users' information and adhering to applicable privacy laws.

How do you communicate these policies with test-takers?

Test-takers can review our privacy policy at any time at Myproctor.ai/privacy-policy. We also communicate our privacy practices to users when they set up an account with us and when they begin an online testing session.

Why does MyProctor need to access any test-taker data?

Our key responsibility is to ensure fairness and integrity in the testing process. We use alternative methods to verify test-taker identity and ensure the testing environment is fair to everyone.

Is MyProctor selling or using test-taker data for other purposes?

No. We only use the data captured during test sessions to conduct online proctoring. MyProctor does not use any test-taker's personal information for any purpose other than facilitating the proctoring of online exams.

What do you do to prevent cheating?

Depending on parameters set by the test provider, we can lock down internet access, monitor video/audio recordings, and use our Live+ solution to monitor in real-time. All measures are disclosed to the test-taker before the test starts.

What happens with data once an exam ends?

Proctoring session data is stored in US-based AES-256 encrypted servers. Recordings are automatically deleted per NIST 800-88 guidelines after two years.

Does MyProctor do anything else with recordings or student information?

No. The information and recordings belong to the testing institution. We do not own the data, don't sell it, and never market to test-takers.

How can test-takers be sure MyProctor is no longer accessing their computer?

Once the LogMeIn applet is closed, the proctor and MyProctor will no longer be able to access the computer or any information on it.

What will my exam experience be like?

MyProctor balances exam security with test-taker experience. Steps may include getting permission to remotely access your computer to ensure no unpermitted resources are running. The test-taker can revoke access at any time.

Where can I find the most recent MyProctor privacy policy?

Visit MyProctor.com/privacy-policy. California residents: CCPA Policy.

Proctoring Process FAQs
What do your proctors watch during an exam?

We look for aberrant behavior such as looking off screen, accessing unpermitted materials, speaking aloud, or allowing other people in the room.

Can proctors access test-taker computers without their knowledge?

No. Test-takers must agree to give permission. All proctor activity can be seen by test-takers in real-time.

Do you switch proctors during Live+ tests?

Sometimes, due to lunch breaks and shift changes. Test-takers are not interrupted and are never left unwatched.

How do proctors stop integrity violations?

During Live+ exams, proctors can communicate with test-takers through webcam. Intervention Specialists are invited if suspicious behavior is suspected.

How does technology help human proctors?

Technology flags activities that might violate integrity requirements. All technology alerts must be verified by a human proctor or exam administrator.

How does MyProctor flag behaviors?

Our system flags and timestamps events into an easy-to-read timeline that allows proctors to click and jump to that moment in the exam recording.

Are exam video recordings always available?

Yes. All videos are kept up to a year unless a different period is requested. Live Look-In lets you watch test-takers in real time.

How do you verify test-taker identities?

Options include public records challenge questions, keystroke biometrics, ID capture, and facial comparison. Any mismatch alerts human proctors.

Do I have to review exam sessions?

With Live+ or Review+, our proctors send reports of sessions needing attention. With Record+, the exam administrator reviews every session.

Can you proctor paper and pencil exams?

We no longer proctor paper-based exams, but we can help you find a test delivery system to transition your program online.
